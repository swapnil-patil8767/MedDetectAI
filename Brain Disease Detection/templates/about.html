How It Works

This application uses a deep learning model based on the Xception architecture to analyze brain MRI scans and detect different types of brain tumors. The model was trained on a dataset of thousands of MRI images and can identify four different categories:

  • Glioma tumors - starting in glial cells
  • Meningioma tumors - arising from the meninges
  • Pituitary tumors - developing in the pituitary gland
  • No tumor - normal brain tissue

When you upload an MRI scan, the system processes the image, normalizes it, and feeds it through the neural network to generate predictions. The results show the most likely diagnosis along with confidence scores for each category.

Technical Details:

  • Base Architecture: Xception (pretrained on ImageNet)
  • Input Size: 299x299 pixels
  • Fine-tuned on Brain Tumor MRI Dataset
  • Classification Categories: 4

About Brain Tumors

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain. They can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Early detection is crucial for effective treatment. Here are the types of tumors this application can detect:

Glioma

Gliomas are tumors that occur in the brain and spinal cord. They begin in the glial cells that surround and support nerve cells. Gliomas can be low-grade (slow growing) or high-grade (fast growing). They represent about 33% of all brain tumors and 80% of all malignant brain tumors.

Symptoms: Headaches, nausea, vomiting, seizures, neurological deficits, and behavioral changes.

Meningioma

Meningiomas arise from the meninges — the membranes that surround the brain and spinal cord. Most meningiomas are benign, slow-growing tumors. They account for about 37% of all primary brain tumors and are most common in women.

Symptoms: Headaches, vision problems, hearing loss, memory loss, seizures, and weakness in extremities.

Pituitary Tumor

Pituitary tumors develop in the pituitary gland at the base of the brain. They can affect hormone production and cause various endocrine disorders. Most pituitary tumors are adenomas (benign). They account for about 10-15% of all brain tumors.

Symptoms: Headaches, vision problems, hormone imbalances, fatigue, changes in menstruation, reduced fertility, and weight gain.
