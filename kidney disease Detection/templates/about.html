How It Works

This application uses a deep learning model to analyze kidney scans and detect different types of kidney diseases. The model was trained on a dataset of kidney images and can identify four different categories:

  • Kidney Cysts
  • Normal Kidney
  • Kidney Stones
  • Kidney Tumors

When you upload a kidney scan, the system processes the image, normalizes it, and feeds it through the neural network to generate predictions. The results show the most likely diagnosis along with confidence scores for each category.

Technical Details:

  • Input Size: 244x244 pixels
  • Fine-tuned on Kidney Disease Dataset
  • Classification Categories: 4

About Kidney Diseases

Kidney diseases are conditions that affect kidney structure and function. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment and preventing complications. Here are the types of conditions this application can detect:

Kidney Cyst

Kidney cysts are fluid-filled sacs that form on or in the kidneys. Simple kidney cysts are usually benign, don't cause symptoms, and rarely require treatment. However, multiple cysts might indicate polycystic kidney disease, a genetic disorder.

Symptoms: Most kidney cysts don't cause symptoms. Larger cysts may cause dull pain in your back or side, fever, or blood in urine.

Kidney Stone

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside your kidneys. They can affect any part of your urinary tract from your kidneys to your bladder. Passing kidney stones can be quite painful, but the stones usually cause no permanent damage.

Symptoms: Severe pain in the side and back, below the ribs, pain that radiates to the lower abdomen and groin, pain that comes in waves and fluctuates in intensity, pink, red or brown urine, cloudy or foul-smelling urine.

Kidney Tumor

Kidney tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Renal cell carcinoma is the most common type of kidney cancer in adults. The exact causes of kidney tumors are not known, but smoking, obesity, and high blood pressure are risk factors.

Symptoms: Blood in urine, persistent back pain just below the ribs, weight loss, fatigue, intermittent fever.
